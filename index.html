About

Hiring strategy redesign
Case Study · Workforce Operations
Cutting onboarding time by 75% at a 100K-person gig ecosystem
Flipkart's last-mile delivery workforce was growing faster than its onboarding process could handle. Background verification and documentation workflows were manual, inconsistent, and creating 24-hour activation delays — directly affecting workforce availability during peak demand cycles like Big Billion Day.
Led a 12-member onboarding COE. Engineered end-to-end automation of BGV and documentation workflows. Activation TAT dropped from 24 hours to 6 hours — becoming a benchmark across the function. Peak cycles (BBD, May Sale) were met with zero workforce shortfall from onboarding delays.
Case Study · Full HR Build · Ciel Et Terre Solar
Zero to structured: Building a full-scale HR function for a global deep-tech solar company scaling India operations
Ciel Et Terre — a French floating solar technology company operating a global Hydrelio® floating structure system — was scaling India operations across Manufacturing, R&D, Engineering, and Construction with no structured HR function. Hiring was reactive and vendor-heavy. Onboarding was ad hoc. L&D didn't exist. Leadership was navigating significant organisational restructuring without change management support. And French expat coordination added a cross-cultural complexity layer that generic HR playbooks don't cover.
Hiring strategy redesign: Rebuilt sourcing strategy and mix across technical and manufacturing functions — significantly reducing external vendor dependency while protecting pipeline quality and hiring timelines.

Structured onboarding: Designed a 30-60-90-day assimilation framework with role-based induction, buddy system, and automated BGV. Extended to cover cross-cultural integration and global assignment documentation for French expats.

OKR-based Performance Management: Rolled out an OKR-linked PMS aligned individual and team goals to business milestones across all functions. Built the calibration and review cycle from scratch.

L&D from zero to full learning culture: Conceived and launched SPARK — a zero-cost internal learning programme starting with MS Excel upskilling — and scaled it to expert-led external sessions. Then led full LMS platform implementation anchored in Training Needs Analysis and competency mapping, establishing the organisation's first structured capability development infrastructure.

Change management & restructuring: Partnered directly with senior leadership through periods of significant org restructuring — providing workforce continuity planning, role clarity frameworks, and internal communication architecture to manage transitions without performance disruption.
Case Study · HR Foundation Build · SRV Infotech
Building an entire HR function from zero — solo — for a growing IT startup with no people infrastructure
SRV Infotech was a growing IT and software firm with no formal HR structure, no documented processes, no policies, and no clarity on how roles related to each other. The company was expanding but operating entirely on informal systems — verbal agreements, ad hoc hiring, undefined accountability. There was one HR hire budgeted: me.
HR Policy Suite & SOPs: Drafted the organisation's complete HR policy documentation — leave, attendance, code of conduct, grievance, disciplinary — compiled into a structured Employee Handbook that created consistent, enforceable people practices for the first time.

Organisation Design: Designed the reporting structure, defined accountability lines, and established a scalable org framework aligned to the company's growth trajectory.

Job Descriptions: Developed role-specific JDs for all key positions — bringing clarity to what each function actually required and enabling more focused, structured hiring.

Hiring Strategy: Built and executed a hiring process from scratch — sourcing approach, interview structure, evaluation criteria — replacing ad hoc recruitment with a repeatable, quality-controlled system.

Onboarding & Offboarding: Designed structured onboarding and exit processes — role-based induction flows, documentation standards, and offboarding checklists — establishing consistent entry and exit experiences for the first time in the organisation.

Employee Engagement Framework: Built an engagement structure that gave employees formal channels for feedback, recognition, and connection — creating the cultural infrastructure that had previously been entirely absent.

Culture & Engagement
Culture isn't built in town halls. It's built in the small decisions no one documents.

The way a manager responds to a mistake. The way an exit is handled. The way feedback is given — or avoided. Trust isn't declared in a values poster. It's either earned or quietly eroded, one interaction at a time. Most orgs measure engagement. Very few measure what's actually driving it.
#Culture #Trust #EmployeeEngagement Read
AI in HR
AI won't replace HR. But HR professionals who don't understand AI will be replaced by those who do.

The question isn't whether AI belongs in HR. It's already there — in screening, scheduling, analytics, and engagement measurement. The real question is: are you driving it, or just watching it happen to your function? The future belongs to HR professionals who think in systems, not just processes.
#HR #AIinHR #FutureOfWork Read
Career & Future of Work
The skills that got you here won't be the ones that take you forward.

Career growth in HR used to mean climbing the generalist ladder. Now it means expanding your fluency — in data, in business, in technology. The HR professionals who will thrive are the ones who can sit in a business review and speak the language of the room, not just translate it back into HR terms later.
#FutureOfWork #AI #CareerGrowth Read
HR Leadership & Work Design
We've redesigned where people work. We haven't redesigned how work is designed.

Hybrid and remote policies got all the attention. But the deeper question — how roles are structured, how decisions are made, how accountability flows across distributed teams — mostly went unanswered. HR leadership today means tackling work design, not just workforce logistics.
#FutureOfWork #HRLeadership #WorkDesign Read
Recruitment & HR Life
Recruitment is the most visible thing HR does. It's also where the most damage gets done quietly.

Bad hires, slow processes, JDs written for imaginary candidates, panels that can't agree on what good looks like — these aren't talent market problems. They're systems problems. And they compound. Every hiring mistake is a lesson in what wasn't designed clearly enough upstream.
#Recruitment #HR #HRLife Read
HR Strategy & Business
HR stops being a cost centre the moment it starts speaking the language of the business.

Revenue, margins, velocity, risk — these aren't finance words. They're the words that matter in every business conversation HR is excluded from. The fastest way to earn a seat at the table is to stop asking for one and start showing up fluent in what the table is actually talking about.
#HR #Strategy #Business Read
Recruiting & Hiring
Candidates remember how they were treated far longer than they remember the role they applied for.

Ghosting after three rounds. Feedback that never comes. Offer timelines that stretch into silence. These aren't just candidate experience failures — they're employer brand failures that compound every time that candidate talks to someone else in the industry.
#Recruiting #HRThings #HR Read
Talent Acquisition
Hiring for the role you have today, not the org you're building tomorrow, is one of the most expensive things a company does.

Succession thinking starts at the first hire, not at the moment you're scrambling to fill a critical leadership gap. The companies that get this right don't just hire — they architect their talent pipeline with the same intentionality they bring to product or revenue.
#Hiring #Recruitment #TalentAcquisition Read
HR Career Growth
No one tells early-career HR professionals that the job is 30% HR knowledge and 70% navigating organisations.

Understanding power structures, earning credibility with sceptical business leaders, influencing without authority, managing up without burning the relationship — these aren't soft skills. They're the actual job. And most HR training programmes skip them entirely.
#Career #Growth #HR Read
Gig Economy & Growth
Managing 100,000+ gig workers taught me something that textbooks don't cover: scale breaks everything you built for smaller numbers.

Policies, processes, communication, onboarding — all of it has to be rethought when you're operating at gig scale. What worked at 500 people becomes a bottleneck at 5,000 and a crisis at 50,000. The lesson: design for the scale you're heading to, not the scale you're at.
#Gig #Freelance #Growth Read
Change Management & Culture
Change management fails when it's treated as a communication exercise rather than a trust exercise.

You can send all the emails and hold all the townhalls. If people don't trust that leadership is being straight with them, none of it lands. The foundation of successful org change isn't the change management plan — it's the credibility that was built (or wasn't) before the change was announced.
#Change #Culture #HR Read
Identity & Belonging
Belonging isn't about being included in the room. It's about whether your perspective changes anything in it.

DEI initiatives that stop at representation miss the point. Diversity is who's at the table. Inclusion is whether they speak. Belonging is whether what they say shifts the outcome. Most organisations are comfortable measuring the first and uncomfortable with the third.
#Identity #Belonging #Inclusion Read
