About

Three environments.
One through-line.

"I don't believe HR is a support function. It's a business lever that's chronically under-deployed."

I'm a SHRM-CP certified HR Business Partner with 5+ years building people systems across three radically different environments: a zero-to-one IT startup where I was the only HR person, Flipkart's 100,000+ gig workforce where scale is the daily challenge, and a global deep-tech solar company where workforce complexity meets cross-cultural coordination.

Most HR professionals operate in one lane. I've been forced to think differently across all three — and that range is what makes me useful to an organisation building something real. I operate at the intersection of business, data, and people — with a bias for impact over process.

If you're looking for an HRBP or Senior HR hire in Bangalore or remote — or a startup navigating its first real HR build — I've been exactly where your problems live.

Workforce Ops · Flipkart
Onboarding Automation at Flipkart — 75% TAT Reduction

Led a 12-member Onboarding COE to automate BGV and documentation workflows end-to-end across Flipkart's 100,000+ gig delivery ecosystem. The 6-hour TAT became a benchmark across the function.

Employer Branding · Research
Employer Branding in Indian Companies — MBA Dissertation

Researched employer branding strategies across Indian organisations for my MBA dissertation at Aligarh Muslim University. Examined how EVP design, talent attraction mechanisms, and employer brand perception drive engagement and retention outcomes. The research explored the gap between how organisations communicate their employer brand externally and how it's experienced internally — a disconnect that drives attrition. Findings directly inform how I approach EVP design and employer brand strategy for clients today, particularly in exit management and talent attraction work.

Watching Movies
Cinema is storytelling at its most concentrated — character, conflict, and resolution compressed into two hours. Good films teach you how humans behave under pressure, which turns out to be directly relevant to HR. Every great character study is a masterclass in motivation.

Travelling
Every new place is a different culture, a different way of organising society, a different set of unspoken rules. Cross-cultural workforce management starts with being genuinely curious about how other people live — and travel is the best education for that.
